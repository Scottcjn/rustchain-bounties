    > CRT LIGHT ATTESTATION

    Security by cathode ray. Absurd almost everywhere else. Perfectly on-brand for RustChain Proof-of-Antiquity.


    > How It Works

    A miner flashes a deterministic pattern on a CRT monitor. A camera or photodiode captures the display, recording scanline timing, phosphor decay, and refresh quirks. That optical fingerprint becomes one more thing emulators can never fake.


    > Why CRTs Are Unforgeable

    • Phosphor decay — LCD/OLED have zero decay (sample-and-hold). Instantly detected.
    • Unique aging — Each CRT ages differently: electron gun wear, phosphor burn-in, flyback drift.
    • No VMs — Virtual machines have no CRT. Period.
    • Scanline jitter — Flyback transformer wear creates unique timing signatures.
    • Brightness curve — Aging electron guns produce nonlinear gamma drift.

    > Phosphor Types

    • P22 — Standard color (RGB tricolor). Consumer TVs and monitors. ~20us decay.
    • P43 — Green (YAG:Ce). Oscilloscopes and radar. ~1000us decay.
    • P4 — White (dual-layer). B&W TV standard. ~60us decay.
    • P31 — Green (zinc silicate). Classic terminal phosphor. ~32us decay.
    • P1 — Green (zinc silicate). Early oscilloscope phosphor. ~24us decay.

    > Integration

    The CRT fingerprint hash is submitted as a crt_fingerprint field in the RustChain attestation payload. On verification, the attestation hash is anchored on-chain, creating a permanent, tamper-proof record of the CRT's optical identity.
