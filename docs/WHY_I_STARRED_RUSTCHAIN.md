## The Problem with Traditional Mining

Most blockchain networks reward participants based on computational speed and energy consumption. This creates an arms race where:
- **Newer hardware dominates**: Vintage systems become obsolete
- **Energy waste is massive**: Billions of kilowatt-hours consumed annually
- **Centralization increases**: Only those who can afford expensive hardware succeed
- **Historical computing heritage is discarded**: Perfectly functional vintage machines end up in landfills

This approach feels fundamentally wrong. Computing history deserves preservation, not obsolescence.

## RustChain's Revolutionary Approach

RustChain flips the mining paradigm on its head with **Proof-of-Antiquity**:

### 1. **Hardware Fingerprinting**
Every miner must prove their hardware is authentic, not emulated. A SheepShaver VM pretending to be a G4 Mac will fail these checks. Real vintage silicon has unique aging patterns that can't be faked.

### 2. **Antiquity Multipliers**
Your hardware's age determines your rewards:
- **PowerPC G4 (1999-2005)**: 2.5× multiplier
- **PowerPC G5 (2003-2006)**: 2.0× multiplier  
- **IBM POWER8 (2014)**: 1.5× multiplier
- **Modern x86_64**: 1.0× baseline

This incentivizes preserving and utilizing vintage hardware rather than discarding it.

### 3. **1 CPU = 1 Vote**
Unlike traditional PoW where hash power equals votes, RustChain uses round-robin consensus. Each unique hardware device gets exactly one vote per epoch, ensuring fair participation regardless of computational speed.
