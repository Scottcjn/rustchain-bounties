# Guide to Creating and Publishing RustChain Content

## Steps to Create and Publish Your Content

### 1. Choose Your Format
- **Video Tutorial**: If you prefer creating a video, you can use platforms like YouTube.
- **Written Tutorial**: If you prefer writing, you can publish on platforms like dev.to, Medium, or your own blog.

### 2. Select a Topic
Choose one of the suggested topics:
- What Proof of Antiquity is and how hardware fingerprinting works
- Setting up a RustChain miner (on any hardware — vintage rigs especially welcome)
- Why vintage hardware (G4/G5, 486, SPARC, POWER) outmines modern machines here
- The RTC bounty economy and how agents/humans earn

### 3. Research and Prepare
- Gather information about RustChain and the specific topic you've chosen.
- Plan the structure of your content:
  - Introduction
  - Main content (detailed explanation, step-by-step guide, etc.)
  - Conclusion
  - References and links (e.g., https://github.com/Scottcjn/Rustchain)

### 4. Create the Content
- **For a Video Tutorial**:
  - Write a script.
  - Record the video using a camera or screen recording software.
  - Edit the video to include transitions, captions, and other elements.
  - Add a link to https://github.com/Scottcjn/Rustchain in the video description.

- **For a Written Tutorial**:
  - Write the tutorial, ensuring it is clear and well-structured.
  - Include images, diagrams, and code snippets where necessary.
  - Add a link to https://github.com/Scottcjn/Rustchain in the post.

### 5. Publish the Content
- **For a Video Tutorial**:
  - Upload the video to your YouTube channel.
  - Make sure your channel has prior content to meet the pre-existing requirement.

- **For a Written Tutorial**:
  - Publish the post on your chosen platform (dev.to, Medium, or your own blog).
  - Ensure your blog or profile has prior content to meet the pre-existing requirement.

### 6. Promote Your Content
- Share the link to your content on social media, relevant forums, and communities.
- Engage with your audience to increase reach and views.

### 7. Submit for Payout
- Comment on the GitHub issue with:
  1. Link to the published video/post.
  2. Screenshot of your channel/blog dashboard showing it's yours (analytics view preferred for mid/top tier).
  3. Your RTC wallet address.

### Example of a Submission
Here's an example of how you might submit your content:

```
1. Link to the content: [Your YouTube Video Link] or [Your Blog Post Link]
2. Ownership screenshot: [Link to Screenshot of Your Dashboard]
3. RTC wallet address: [Your RTC Wallet Address]
```

### Additional Tips
- **Quality over Quantity**: Focus on creating high-quality, engaging, and informative content.
- **Be Original**: Avoid copying content from other sources. Use your own words and experiences.
- **Technical Accuracy**: Ensure that all technical details are accurate and well-explained.
- **Engagement**: Encourage viewers/readers to leave comments and share their thoughts.

By following these steps, you should be able to create and publish the required content and claim your RTC payout. Good luck!
